Jessica Ashman is a London based artist working in moving image, painting, music (under the name Spirit Sigh) performance and installation. Ashman combines drawn and collage animation techniques with live sonic landscapes, creating frenetic yet harmonic abstract narratives that explore gender, identity and race. She is influenced by concepts of ‘the personal archive’, drawing on wider stories of the Black British diaspora communities she was raised in, as well black radical feminist theory and science fiction.

A Royal College of Art graduate, her work has been supported by Arts Council England, New Art Exchange, 198 Contemporary and Focal Points Gallery.
