Friday 27th October

Artist workshops with Khabat Abas, Jessica Ashman, and CeReNeM researchers.

All workshops are free to attend.

  • CeReNeM

    Introducing the Fluid Corpus Manipulation toolkit for Max, Puredata & SuperCollider ( for 16+ )

    15.00 - 16.30

    You will be introduced to the Fluid Corpus Manipulation toolkit (flucoma.org ↗(opens in new tab)), a free set of tools developed at the University of Huddersfield. CeReNeM explains the theoretical concepts of machine listening and machine learning, demonstrating to get it running with your coding environment: Max, PureData or SuperCollider. bring your own laptop with Max, Pd or SuperCollider & headphones.

    Some experience with either max, pd or supercollider is necessary.

    Limited to 12 participants

    Sign up on Eventbrite ↗(opens in new tab)

  • Jessica Ashman

    Ancestral Animation Soundscapes ( for 16+ )

    16.30 - 18.00

    You'll have the opportunity to create your own short animated GIFs inspired by an ancestral image or memory of your own choosing. The workshop will culminate in a collaborative live soundtrack to your animations, in a (relaxed!) group music performance. Artist, Jessica Ashman, will guide you through simple animation, visual sound scoring and soundscape techniques, reflecting on elements of her artistic and music practice.

    Bring a photo, image or object you feel connects to your ancestry, culture or family memory - this could be a family photo, photos of friends, photos of an event, an object, postcards, magazine cut outs or any visual image you connect to. The photos/objects/images will not be damaged. If you are musically inclined, bring in instruments or sound making objects along.

    No experience necessary.

    Limited to 10 participants

    Sign up on Eventbrite ↗(opens in new tab)

  • Khabat Abas

    Breaking Rules ( for 16+ )

    18.30 - 20.00

    This workshop aims to explore and push boundaries in sound through experimentation and modification of music instruments and way of playing to explore new perspectives of making sound. We will critically reflect on the role of the performer and examine the knowledge underlying instruments or objects. The workshop will include the processes of practice, discussions and challenge and explore different modes of performance. Bring your musical instrument and an object that you associate with a memory.

    Some experience playing a musical instrument is necessary.

    Limited to 10 participants
