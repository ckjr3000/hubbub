Artists

Winter Process invited two artists-in-residence, KP Culver and Onyee Lo, to collaborate with local artisans Handmade Bakery, and Zapato Brewing.

  • KP Culver(opens in new tab)

    KP Culver is researching into the once popular folk songs of 'John Barleycorn' in which the beer making process is personified by the song's protagonist. There have been many versions and more contemporary uses of the name, however in older texts and more pagan readings ‘John Barleycorn’ ultimately represents the spirit of grain; harvested, fermented and sown the following year. With a focus on pubs as communal spaces infused with music and storytelling, Culver will be creating a new work titled ‘Oh, Johnny’.

  • Onyee Lo(opens in new tab)

    During this residency with HMB, OnYee looks into map out what the act of baking can inform us about allyship as a facilitator and collaborator, using a set of shared vocabularies unpicked from the “bread language” - as a dutiful response to the current time we live in, in the form of an ever-developing testimony and solidarity

    Communal Sounds Workshop with KP Culver

    In this workshop we will explore noise that originates from what is happening in a space, and does not always need to be in relation with each other. From the hum of speaking in low tones to glasses clinking or seats adjusting. We will use our voice, bodies and objects to create a short sound piece. We will go through the work that has been created for the exhibition whilst we break bread, plus some communal singing.
